## 🎯 Prompt Template: Blog Post (SEO-Optimized)

### Long-Form Content

```
Write an SEO-optimized blog post:

Topic: {topic}
Primary keyword: {main_keyword}
Secondary keywords: {related_keywords}
Target audience: {audience}
Word count: {1500-2000}
Intent: {informational/commercial/transactional}

Structure:
1. Title (H1) - Include primary keyword
2. Introduction (150-200 words) - Hook + keyword
3. Main sections (H2) - 3-5 sections
4. Subsections (H3) - Supporting points
5. Conclusion (100-150 words) - Summary + CTA
6. FAQ section (optional)

Requirements:
- Primary keyword in first 100 words
- Keyword density: 1-2%
- Include internal links (3-5)
- Include external links (2-3 authoritative sources)
- Meta description (150-160 chars)
- Alt text for images
```

### Example Usage

**Input:**
```
Topic: How to Care for Roses
Primary keyword: rose care tips
Secondary keywords: rose maintenance, keep roses fresh, rose watering
Target audience: Home gardeners, flower enthusiasts
Word count: 1500
Intent: Informational
```

**Output:**
```

## 1. Watering: The Foundation of Rose Care

### How Much Water Do Roses Need?

Roses require consistent moisture but hate "wet feet." The key to proper 
rose watering is deep, infrequent watering rather than shallow, frequent 
sprinkles.

**Best practices:**
- Water deeply 2-3 times per week
- Provide 1-2 inches of water per session
- Water at the base, not overhead
- Morning watering prevents fungal diseases

### Signs of Overwatering vs. Underwatering

**Overwatering symptoms:**
- Yellow leaves
- Root rot
- Wilting despite wet soil

**Underwatering symptoms:**
- Drooping flowers
- Brown leaf edges
- Slow growth

## 2. Sunlight Requirements

Roses are sun-loving plants that need 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily. 
Position your roses where they receive morning sun, which helps dry dew 
and prevents disease.

**Pro tip:** In hot climates like Vietnam, afternoon shade can prevent 
heat stress while still providing adequate light.

## 3. Soil and Fertilization

### Ideal Soil Conditions

Roses thrive in well-draining soil with:
- pH level: 6.0-6.5 (slightly acidic)
- Rich organic matter
- Good drainage

### Fertilization Schedule

- **Spring:** Apply balanced fertilizer (10-10-10)
- **Summer:** Feed every 4-6 weeks during growing season
- **Fall:** Reduce feeding to prepare for dormancy
- **Winter:** No fertilization needed

## 4. Pruning for Health and Beauty

Regular pruning is essential for rose maintenance. Remove:
- Dead or diseased wood
- Crossing branches
- Spent blooms (deadheading)

**When to prune:**
- Major pruning: Late winter/early spring
- Deadheading: Throughout blooming season
- Light shaping: As needed

## 5. Pest and Disease Prevention

### Common Rose Pests

- Aphids
- Spider mites
- Japanese beetles

### Natural Prevention Methods

1. Companion planting (garlic, marigolds)
2. Neem oil spray
3. Regular inspection
4. Proper spacing for air circulation

## 6. Seasonal Care Adjustments

### Spring
- Prune dead wood
- Apply fresh mulch
- Begin fertilization

### Summer
- Increase watering frequency
- Deadhead regularly
- Monitor for pests

### Fall
- Reduce watering
- Stop fertilizing
- Prepare for dormancy

### Winter
- Protect from frost (if applicable)
- Minimal watering
- No pruning

## 7. Cut Rose Care (Bouquets)

If you've received roses as a gift, follow these tips to keep them fresh:

1. **Trim stems at 45° angle** - Increases water absorption
2. **Remove lower leaves** - Prevents bacterial growth
3. **Use clean vase** - Wash with soap and water
4. **Change water daily** - Keeps bacteria at bay
5. **Add flower food** - Provides nutrients
6. **Keep cool** - Away from direct sunlight and heat

**Expected vase life:** 7-10 days with proper care

## 8. Container Rose Care

Growing roses in pots requires special attention:

- **Pot size:** Minimum 15-20 inches deep
- **Drainage:** Essential for container roses
- **Watering:** More frequent than ground roses
- **Fertilization:** Every 2-3 weeks (diluted)

## 9. Troubleshooting Common Problems

### Yellow Leaves
**Causes:** Overwatering, nutrient deficiency, disease
**Solution:** Adjust watering, apply fertilizer, check for pests

### Black Spots
**Causes:** Fungal disease
**Solution:** Remove affected leaves, improve air circulation, apply 
fungicide

### No Blooms
**Causes:** Insufficient sunlight, over-pruning, nutrient imbalance
**Solution:** Relocate to sunnier spot, adjust fertilization

## 10. Professional Rose Care Services

For busy homeowners or special occasions, consider professional rose 
care services:

- Regular maintenance visits
- Seasonal pruning
- Pest and disease treatment
- Fertilization programs

At [Flower Shop Name], we offer comprehensive rose care consultations 
and maintenance packages. [Internal link to services page]

## Conclusion

Mastering rose care tips doesn't happen overnight, but with consistent 
attention to watering, sunlight, soil, and pruning, your roses will 
reward you with stunning blooms season after season. Remember that rose 
maintenance is about observation—your roses will tell you what they need 
if you pay attention.

Ready to start your rose garden? Browse our selection of premium rose 
varieties perfect for Vietnamese climate. [Internal link to rose category]

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should I water roses?**
A: Water deeply 2-3 times per week, providing 1-2 inches of water per 
session. Adjust based on weather and soil conditions.

**Q: Can roses grow in pots?**
A: Yes! Choose containers at least 15-20 inches deep with good drainage. 
Container roses need more frequent watering and fertilization.

**Q: When is the best time to prune roses?**
A: Major pruning should be done in late winter or early spring before 
new growth begins. Deadheading can be done throughout the blooming season.

**Q: Why are my rose leaves turning yellow?**
A: Yellow leaves can indicate overwatering, nutrient deficiency, or 
disease. Check soil moisture and consider applying balanced fertilizer.

**Q: How long do cut roses last?**
A: With proper care (clean vase, daily water changes, stem trimming), 
cut roses typically last 7-10 days.
```

```
Write SEO-optimized category page content:

Category: {category_name}
Primary keyword: {main_keyword}
Location: {city/region}
Product count: {number}

Structure:
1. H1: Category name + keyword
2. Introduction (100-150 words)
3. Why choose this category (2-3 benefits)
4. Popular products (brief mentions)
5. Buying guide (3-5 tips)
6. FAQ (3-5 questions)

Requirements:
- Keyword in H1, first paragraph, and naturally throughout
- Include location for local SEO
- Link to related categories
- Meta description
```

## 📋 SEO Content Checklist

### On-Page SEO
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1
- [ ] Keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Keyword density 1-2%
- [ ] H2/H3 subheadings with keywords
- [ ] Meta description (150-160 chars)
- [ ] Alt text for all images
- [ ] Internal links (3-5)
- [ ] External links (2-3)
- [ ] URL slug with keyword

### Content Quality
- [ ] Original, valuable content
- [ ] Answers user intent
- [ ] Proper grammar and spelling
- [ ] Scannable (short paragraphs, bullets)
- [ ] Mobile-friendly formatting
- [ ] Clear call-to-action

### Technical SEO
- [ ] Schema markup (Article, Product, FAQ)
- [ ] Canonical URL
- [ ] Open Graph tags
- [ ] Twitter Card tags
- [ ] Page speed optimized
